# 05 — E&O / Professional Liability Insurance (research & action doc)

> ⚠️ **Not insurance advice.** First-draft research to guide a conversation with a licensed
> broker. Coverage, limits, exclusions, and pricing are determined by the carrier and your
> actual risk profile. 2026 estimates.

---

## 1. Coverage shape — what to buy
**Technology E&O bundled with Cyber, written as professional liability with an
attestation/verification scope.** Two exposures stack:

- **Professional liability (the core):** you issue a verdict a counterparty **relies on** near
  a capital decision. That reliance is the risk, and it resembles an **auditor/attestation**
  profile more than generic IT consulting.
- **Cyber/data:** you ingest a counterparty's **code and data**, so a breach or leak is a real
  exposure even though you don't retain it. Cyber covers breach response, notification, and
  liability.

Optional later: **D&O** (directors & officers) once you raise — investors often require it.
Startup brokers bundle Tech E&O + Cyber + D&O.

## 2. Limits to start
- **$1M per claim / $2M aggregate** is the sensible starting point.
- Allocator/fund clients may **require $1M–$2M** before engaging — ask early; a contractual
  insurance requirement is common in diligence vendor agreements.
- Small attestation/accounting-style firms commonly carry **$1M/$2M up to $2M/$4M**.

## 3. Cost ranges (estimate — refine with quotes)
- Generic solo tech E&O ($1M): **~$1,000–$3,000/yr**.
- Because this is **opinion/attestation work others rely on for money**, expect **higher and
  more scrutinized: ~$2,000–$6,000+/yr** for $1M/$2M.
- **At the very start** — novel service, no track record, third-party-reliance exposure — it may
  be **harder to place** or come with **exclusions/sub-limits** until you have a few engagements
  logged. Budget for iteration and lead time.

## 4. Underwriter questionnaire — be ready to answer
- **Services description** (independent computational verification; reproducibility-only).
- **Projected revenue** and **expected number of engagements** per year.
- **Written contracts?** → *Yes.* "We use a written engagement letter with a **liability cap**
  and **disclaimers** on every engagement." *(Biggest premium lever — show `03` and `04`.)*
- **Do you provide investment or securities advice / recommendations?** → **No.** "We certify
  reproducibility, not investment merit; we are not an investment adviser or auditor." *(Keeps
  you in a coverable class and clear of investment-advice exclusions.)*
- **Data handling/security:** isolated, network-denied execution; **no retention beyond the run**;
  no host access. *(Lowers cyber risk.)*
- **Client types:** funds, allocators, ODD/seeders.
- **Prior claims/circumstances:** none.
- **Principal background** and any subcontractors.
- **Largest single engagement fee / contract size** (drives limit needs).

## 5. How your contract terms lower premium / risk
Underwriters reward exactly the posture Calma already wants:
- **Hard liability cap** (fees paid, or a small multiple) — caps the carrier's exposure.
- **Explicit disclaimers** — reproducibility-only, deterministic verdict, **no reliance beyond
  the named client**, no investment advice.
- **Single, defined deliverable** per engagement — bounded scope.
- **Prepaid + non-contingent** terms — no incentive to please the verified party, reducing the
  conflict-of-interest narrative.
- **Isolated, no-retention data handling** — reduces the cyber/breach tail.

Bring `03` (engagement letter) and `04` (disclaimer) to the broker — they are underwriting
assets, not just legal docs.

## 6. Exclusions / watch-outs to raise with the broker
- Many tech-E&O forms **exclude "financial/investment advice" and "express guarantees/
  warranties."** Confirm your **verification opinions are covered** and that your disclaimers keep
  you clear of the investment-advice exclusion.
- Watch for **professional-services definitions** that don't contemplate attestation/verification
  — get the scope endorsed to match what you actually do.
- Confirm **third-party reliance** is handled the way your contracts intend (reliance limited to
  the named client — your contracts should match the policy's assumptions).
- Check **prior-acts / retroactive date** and that **defense costs** are inside or outside the
  limit.

## 7. Where to get quotes (get 2–3)
- **Startup-savvy brokers:** **Vouch**, **Embroker** — both bundle Tech E&O + Cyber (+ D&O) and
  understand novel tech services.
- **Small-business:** **Insureon**, **The Hartford** (fast tech E&O quotes).
- **Professional-liability / attestation specialists** (if carriers balk at the attestation
  angle): the accountants'-PL market — **CAMICO, Berkley, CFC, The Hartford** auditors' PI.

## 8. Action plan
1. Finalize `03` + `04` with counsel (your underwriting assets).
2. Request quotes from **2 startup brokers + 1 professional-liability specialist**, leading with
   the **cap + scope + no-investment-advice** posture and the **isolated/no-retention** handling.
3. Bind **$1M/$2M Tech E&O + Cyber** before engagement #1.
4. Revisit limits and add **D&O** at fundraise.
