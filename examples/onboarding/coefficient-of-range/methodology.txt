The coefficient of range is a unit-free measure of statistical dispersion: the range of a sample
(its maximum minus its minimum) divided by the sum of the maximum and the minimum.

For strictly positive data it lies between 0 and 1 -- it is 0 when every value is identical and
approaches 1 as the spread widens. It is invariant to the unit the data is measured in (scaling every
value by the same positive constant leaves it unchanged) and it does not depend on the order of the
observations.
